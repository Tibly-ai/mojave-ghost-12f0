What's Included

Everything the desert asks you to slow down for

We've spent three years filling Mojave Ghost with the things that make a desert stay worth remembering: good linens, a kitchen you'll actually cook in, and a fire pit that stays lit past midnight. Nothing here is fussy. It's just chosen carefully, so you can stop thinking about logistics and start noticing the light.

Comfort Inside

  • Linen bedding and blackout curtains
  • Evaporative cooling for warm afternoons
  • Wood stove for cold desert nights
  • Fast Wi-Fi throughout the house

Kitchen & Gathering

  • Full kitchen, cast iron and a French press
  • Farmhouse table that seats eight
  • Local honey and coffee left for arrival
  • Outdoor dining spot under the ramada

Desert Evenings

  • Private fire pit with wood always stacked
  • String lights over the back patio
  • Cast iron for roasting marshmallows or dinner
  • Wool blankets for sitting out late

Stargazing Deck

  • Private rooftop deck, no light pollution
  • Tabletop telescope and star chart
  • Two low lounge chairs and a daybed
  • Best viewing runs April through October

Bath & Body

  • Rainfall shower and deep clawfoot tub
  • Handmade sage soap from a shop in town
  • Waffle robes and extra towels
  • Hair dryer and full-length mirror

Outdoor Living

  • Hammock strung between two mesquite posts
  • Shaded ramada for the hottest part of the day
  • Outdoor shower, rinse the trail dust off
  • Gravel yard fenced for pets to roam

How a Stay Here Actually Works

No front desk, no fuss. Here's what to expect from the moment you arrive.

1

Self Check-In

We text your lockbox code the morning of arrival. Pull in whenever suits you after 3pm.

2

Settle In

A house guide, a welcome basket, and the thermostat already set for the season.

3

Light the Fire

Wood is stacked and ready every evening. Matches live in the tin by the back door.

4

Look Up

Head to the deck after dark. The telescope's set up, the sky does the rest.
